You're hosting and producing at the same time. Listening to your guest, thinking about the next question, watching the clock. Your guest drops a detail that would make a perfect clip — and you're too deep in the conversation to write it down.

After the call, you scrub through forty minutes of audio looking for the eight seconds you half-remember. Or worse, you don't look at all. The hook dies in the recording.

You don't need another post-production tool. You need a sidebar. Podruff runs in your browser while you record. It watches the conversation and fills a live feed with hooks, topic flags, and follow-up questions. You glance at it the way you'd glance at a producer's notes through the glass.

How it works

01

Open the sidebar

Point Podruff at your call audio and open it in a browser tab. Dock it alongside Zoom, Riverside, SquadCast — whatever you record with.

02

Watch the feed fill up

As you talk, hooks, topic flags, and follow-up questions stream into the sidebar. Color-coded. Timestamped. Newest at the top. Glance over when you want a nudge.

03

Mark, save, or ignore

Tap a suggestion to mark it as used. Save the good ones for post. Skip the rest. When you're done, everything exports to Markdown — timestamped, tagged, ready for show notes.

Hooks

Quotable soundbites, live

Under ten words. Punchy. Specific. Podruff generates 2-3 hook ideas every few seconds from what's being said. Not summaries — the kind of one-liner you'd put in a clip caption or episode title. They show up in the sidebar as blue cards, timestamped and ready to use.

Topics

Threads worth pulling

Your guest keeps circling back to a comparison, or drops a tension they don't resolve. Podruff flags it as a topic card — a thread that's worth exploring before you move on. Think of it as a producer tapping the glass: "stay on this one."

Follow-ups

The question you forgot to ask

They mentioned a co-founder disagreement and kept talking. You were nodding. Podruff was writing down the follow-up: "Ask what the argument was about." It surfaces the questions you'd think of in the shower two hours later — except now they're on your screen mid-call.
