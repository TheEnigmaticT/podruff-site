You're hosting and producing at the same time. Listening to your guest, thinking about the next question, watching the clock, and trying to remember which story had the better hook. Something always gets dropped.

Your guest mentions a detail that would make a perfect clip. But you're mid-conversation and there's no one on the other side of the glass to scribble "go back to that." So you don't. You move on. The moment's gone.

Solo podcasters don't need another editing tool. They need a producer. Podruff watches the conversation in real time and tells you what to ask next, which hooks need sharpening, and when you're sitting on a better story than the one you're telling.

How it works

01

Route your audio

Point Podruff at your call's audio output. Works with Zoom, Riverside, SquadCast — anything with a mic signal.

02

Start talking

Have your conversation. Podruff listens in real time and surfaces follow-up questions, hook rewrites, and angle suggestions as they come up. Glance over when you need a nudge.

03

Use what lands

Some suggestions you'll use mid-conversation. Others you'll save for the edit. Either way, you walked out of that recording with better material than you would have alone.

Follow-ups

It catches what you miss

Your guest drops a detail and keeps talking. You're focused on the next question. Podruff isn't. It flags the thread you should pull on — the half-told story, the glossed-over conflict, the number they didn't finish — and puts a follow-up question on your screen before the moment passes.

Hook rewrites

Sharper hooks, suggested live

Your guest says "it was really hard." That's not a clip. Podruff hears the same thing and suggests: "Push for the specifics — what made it hard, and when did they almost quit?" You get the version that stops a scroller. The one that would've taken three re-listens to find in post.
