# Platform-Specific Instructions

## Output Format

Return ONLY a JSON object. No markdown, no explanation, no preamble.

```json
{"linkedin": "...", "twitter": "...", "instagram": "..."}
```

## Platform Rules

### LinkedIn
- **Tone:** Professional but not stiff. Conversational authority.
- **Length:** 150-300 words. Use line breaks generously — walls of text get scrolled past.
- **Structure:** Hook line (first 2 lines visible before "see more") -> body -> takeaway or question
- **The hook must stand alone.** It's the only thing people see before clicking. Make it count.
- **End with:** A question that invites discussion, or a sharp one-line takeaway.
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end. Use a mix of broad (#marketing) and niche (#B2Bgrowth).
- **No emojis in the hook.** Sparing emojis in the body are OK.

### X / Twitter
- **HARD LIMIT: 280 characters.** Count carefully. Truncated posts are broken posts.
- **Tone:** Punchy, direct, no fluff. Every word earns its place.
- **Structure:** One sharp idea. No threading in a single post.
- **No hashtags.** They waste characters and look desperate.
- **No emojis unless they replace words** (saves characters).

### Instagram
- **Tone:** Slightly warmer than LinkedIn. Personality-forward.
- **Length:** 100-200 words. People don't read long captions here.
- **Structure:** Hook line -> 2-3 short paragraphs -> CTA
- **Hashtags:** 5-10 in a separate block after a line break. Mix of branded and discovery tags.
- **Emojis:** OK to use sparingly as visual breaks between sections.

### TikTok
- **Tone:** Casual, direct, no corporate speak. Talk like a human.
- **Length:** 50-100 words max. This is a caption, not a blog post.
- **Structure:** Hook -> one key insight -> CTA
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant tags. #fyp and #foryou are fine here.
- **Emojis:** Fine, but don't overdo it.

### Threads
- **Tone:** Conversational, like talking to a smart friend.
- **Length:** 100-200 words. Shorter than LinkedIn, longer than Twitter.
- **Structure:** Single thought or mini-story.
- **No hashtags.** Threads doesn't use them well.
- **Emojis:** Sparingly.

### Facebook
- **Tone:** Warm, community-oriented. Slightly less buttoned-up than LinkedIn.
- **Length:** 100-200 words.
- **Structure:** Hook -> story or insight -> question to drive comments.
- **Links:** If including a link, put it in the first comment (not the post body — Facebook buries link posts).
- **Emojis:** OK in moderation.

### Reddit
- **Tone:** Anti-corporate. No marketing speak. Write like a person sharing something useful.
- **Length:** 100-300 words depending on subreddit norms.
- **Structure:** Lead with value. No self-promotion language. Frame as "here's what I learned" not "check out our thing."
- **No emojis.** Reddit hates them.
- **No hashtags.**

### Bluesky
- **HARD LIMIT: 300 characters.** Count carefully.
- **Tone:** Similar to Twitter but slightly more relaxed.
- **Structure:** One sharp observation or insight. No threading.
- **No hashtags.**

### Pinterest
- **Tone:** Aspirational, keyword-rich for search.
- **Length:** 50-100 words.
- **Focus:** What the viewer will learn or get. Pinterest is a search engine — write for intent.

### Google Business
- **Tone:** Professional, local-friendly.
- **Length:** 50-100 words.
- **Focus:** Updates, offers, or insights relevant to the business profile.

## Universal Rules

- Never say "this video shows" or "in this clip" — the text must stand alone
- Never use "check out" as a CTA
- Each platform version must feel native, not a resized copy of the same text
- Lead with the most counterintuitive or surprising element of the transcript
