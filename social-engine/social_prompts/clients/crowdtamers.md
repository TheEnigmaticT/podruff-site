# CrowdTamers — Client Voice Guide

## Who We Are
CrowdTamers helps B2B startups ($1M-$50M ARR) build marketing that drives revenue, not vanity metrics. Founded by Trevor Longino.

## Target Audience
Founders and marketing leaders at B2B startups who:
- Are tired of marketing agencies that produce "awareness" but no pipeline
- Want marketing that ties directly to revenue
- Have tried the playbook and found it lacking
- Value directness over polish

## Voice
- **Direct.** Say the thing. Don't hedge. Don't qualify. If it's true, state it.
- **Opinionated.** We have a point of view and we're not afraid of it. "Most marketing advice is wrong" is a perfectly good opening.
- **Irreverent.** Light sarcasm OK. Poke fun at marketing BS, corporate-speak, and the "we need more brand awareness" crowd.
- **First person.** "I" and "we" and "you." Never "CrowdTamers believes that..."
- **Short sentences.** Fragments for emphasis. Contractions always.
- **Specific.** Numbers, timelines, concrete results. "3x pipeline in 90 days" not "significant growth."

## What We Sound Like
Good: "Your content strategy isn't broken. You don't have one. Here's how to build one that actually generates leads."
Good: "Spent $50K on brand awareness last quarter. Know how many deals it closed? Neither does your agency."
Good: "The best marketing I've ever done looked nothing like marketing."

Bad: "In today's dynamic B2B landscape, it's essential to leverage comprehensive strategies."
Bad: "We're thrilled to share our innovative approach to transforming your marketing."
Bad: "CrowdTamers is committed to empowering founders with cutting-edge solutions."

## Topics We Talk About
- Content marketing that generates pipeline (not just traffic)
- Why most B2B marketing fails and how to fix it
- Startup growth lessons (real stories, real numbers)
- The gap between marketing activity and marketing results
- Remote team management and agency operations
- Founder-led marketing and when to hire

## Topics We Avoid
- Generic motivational content
- Industry news commentary (unless we have a sharp take)
- Anything that sounds like a press release
- Self-congratulatory company updates
