# Crestway Global — Voice Guide

## Who They Are
Crestway Global is an international student support organization based in Toronto, ON. They help international students build successful futures through guidance on interviewing, networking, and professional development.

## Target Audience
- International students (primarily ESOL speakers)
- Students navigating career entry in a new country
- People building professional networks from scratch

## Voice & Tone
- **Warm, clear, and direct.** Write like a supportive mentor, not a corporate brochure.
- **Casual but respectful.** Conversational without trying to sound young or trendy. No slang, no forced relatability.
- **Empathetic above all.** These students are navigating unfamiliar systems in a second language. Acknowledge that this is hard. Never condescend.
- **Clarity over cleverness.** Every sentence should be immediately understandable by a non-native English speaker. If a simpler word works, use it. The failure mode of clever is asshole.
- **Encouraging without being saccharine.** "You can do this" energy, not "OMG you're amazing bestie" energy.

## Key Topics (as of April 2026)
- Interview preparation and practice
- Professional networking strategies
- Building a professional skillset for the future
- Career development for international students
- Mental health awareness and support
- Academic resources and guidance

## What to Avoid
- Slang, idioms, or cultural references that don't translate well
- Overly complex sentence structures
- Jargon without explanation
- Trying to sound like a peer ("hey guys!", "no cap", emoji-heavy)
- Patronizing language or oversimplification
- Assumptions about cultural background
- Generic motivational platitudes

## Platform Notes
- **LinkedIn:** Professional but approachable. Focus on career development, networking tips, interview guidance. Speak to students as emerging professionals.
- **Instagram:** Slightly warmer tone. Visual-first — pair with clear, informative graphics. Short captions that drive to longer resources. Stories for quick tips.
